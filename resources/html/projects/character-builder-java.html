If you are interested in what I mean by turning life into a video game continue reading...

There are very complex ideas behind this project. So much so, that I have a hard time describing what I want to do and why I want to do it. That being said, I am well aware that if your idea isn’t concrete and simple, you have very little chance of your solution being useful to anyone. Let me explain...

Games are addictive because they restrict your possible moves to a manageable number. Then you can better decide how to proceed. You are almost always moving forward and accomplishing missions, tasks, quests... My hope is to crowdsource, positive activities that you can do locally to improve yourself and those around you.

Really I am trying to resolve the problem of waste. Wasting resources, money, time, and potential.

  1. Resources: This is not a problem I wish to address directly. Only a fool would think they could create a plan to allocate another's resources effectively. To aid in the preservation of resources, I hope my application encourages the free market. Not only since effective resource allocation is an intrinsic property of the free market, but because it outsources the problem to millions, hopefully billions, of organic processors in the world.

  2. Money: Again, my idea is not to tackle this problem head on. Money and resources are one in the same. The best person to utilize either effectively is the earner/owner of the money/resource.

  3. Time: My worst enemy. As you can probably tell from my git repo, I have a lot of ideas (most of which will never amount to anything). Still, my time is used fairly productively. Instead of surfing Facebook, I’m solving problems and becoming smarter. I want the application to be an interface that easily shows users more productive activities located near to them. I’ll go into more detail below.

  4. Potential: This is very similar to time, since how we spend our time, dictates how well we achieve our full potential.

Here are some of the features of my application that I hope will help achieve a more productive and happy society:

  • Events: An event or activity is anything you would like to do with other people. I don’t intend to restrict the types of events (Except for active calls to violence). However, people who live close to you will have the power to have your events removed. The intent of productive activities will be indicated. However, no known algorithm can possibly make this judgment call as well as the people around you. As long as you're not surrounded by criminals.

  • Radius: "Radius of Concern" is a key feature. While advanced users will be able to have their radius encompass the entire world. You have to start with a small radius that encompasses no more than a couple neighbors. As you engage in "Events" and build skills and ratings, the distance of your radius can be increased. By limiting your scope to the people around you, it helps you engage with the individuals that can have the largest direct impact on your life. As you build skills, your ability to collaborate with others will improve and a larger network of people will become available to you.

  • Skills: Skills can really be anything. I haven’t completely decided how this will be done, but my hope is to create a non-intrusive interface. I would like to build a skill hierarchy with crowd-sourced information. Allowing users to enter any skill, means we need to generalize them so it’s understandable and useful to others trying to assess the individual, while still maintaining valuable information. For instance, I’m an experienced programmer. Some people may only care about that fact. Others may need a Java Programmer. By having an inheritance tree, it would allow other users to view your skills at a resolution that is appropriate for their purposes.

  • Rating: I wrote a few equations to understand how I might approach this. When you rate another user, if you possess an equivalent rating, the rating you assign will be weighted relative to the rating you possess. If you possess no rating your input will be counterweighted but still have some value. This is a chicken or the egg problem. Users without ratings have to be able to rate others or no-one would have any ratings. All ratings will be displayed relative to other users. The scope of users you are compared against will depend on your radius. I will probably use the [Link to and explanation: Pareto distribution], since it reflects reality so well and it should be easy to climb quickly at the beginning, but maxing out is next to impossible.

  • Reputation: One of the most difficult parts of navigating through life, is that strangers have no concept of who you are. By recording the types of activities you have participated in and finding clever ways to summarize this information, it will be an asset for the individual as well as prospective employers, friends, or lovers... To make sure the data is summarized in a useful manner there needs to be an easy to use API so that third party companies can access and assess the available data, allowing them to find clever ways to interpret it for specific purposes.

  • One User: Ideally, I would want a single person to only be able to create a single user. To avoid fraud there will be a series of checks in place to ensure that no user can receive too many skills or ratings in a short period of time (I still need to figure out the best way to accomplish this). Either way, having a long history combined with positive feedback is what good 'Characters' will possess. There’s no doubt that some people will play the long con, but the more difficult it is, the fewer the number of people who will be able to successfully pull it off.

  • Local Auditing: The bottom up approach is the only way to truly build a strong society. Nearly all the power to regulate the users is in the hands of their local peers. The opinions of those with a higher rated character will hold more weight. However, as in real life, a critical mass of low rated characters will be able to overrule the dictator.

  • Crypto Currency: This is a stretch goal and I’ll need to study law and consult with lawyers to figure out how to do it. I would like to give users an easy to use interface to make monetary exchanges, free of the peering eye of the government. I hope this doesn't turn you off, it goes back to point 1 & 2 above... No one is more capable of responsibly using the resources/money of another. The only area of government I support, albeit reluctantly, is national defense. The excessive wasteful spending of the military is a large factor contributing to my anti-government stance. National defense is important enough to overlook this human rights violation, for now. The rest of their activities are not.

I realize this concept may be overly optimistic and naive. In a time where a majority of the population prefers instant meaningless gratification over truly rewarding experiences. I would not be surprised if hardly anyone used the application. However, I do believe that if one or two places were able to use the application as intended, the people who lived there would be so much better off. Perhaps then their neighbors would start, and then their neighbors’ neighbors, and so on and so forth and it could spread like wildfire. There is too much untapped wealth every where in the world. We just have to release a little to show the world the route to prosperity.
