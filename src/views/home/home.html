Chaos
here Introduction

As you may have guessed the theme of this website is the relationship between chaos and order. I dont know of any aspect of reality that cannot be described as such, when viewed from the right angle. Over the past few years, I have come to realize that straddling the line between chaos and order is fundamentally, what life is all about.

Taming the Chaos

I think everyone enjoys bringing chaos to order, whether your organizing, fixing, or cleaning. It feels good to take something from an undesirable state to a desirable one. For me it’s almost like a junkie chasing a high.

Cleaning
When I was very young I enjoyed cleaning. As I got better at cleaning, the size of the job had to get bigger and messier in order for me to feel fulfilled. I remember several occasions when I would stay up all night and clean the entire downstairs floor of my house. If I cleaned while everyone was awake somebody might help and that wouldn't feel as good as doing everything myself.

Math Math was another passion when I was younger. It’s one of the few subjects in the world, maybe the only one, where the only chaos that exists is within your own mind. I find the concept of that soothing. I think I latched on to math, and later programming, to wrestle the chaos within my own mind.

Wood Working
Woodworking will always be a passion of mine, although I enjoy the process of design far more than the process of constructing. Designing furniture is just creativity combined with applied mathematics. The chaos here is created by the needs, or desires, of people. How well your furniture turns out is a measurement of how well you were able to tame the chaos. Hopefully, later in life I, will be able to combine my programming knowledge with machines and automate many of the tedious aspects of our existence.

Programming
Programming is the ideal activity for me to fully enjoy my love of design and perfection. If we ignore hardware failure, a program will do exactly as I tell it to do. Again, I prefer and enjoy certainty. Even hardware failure can be predicted and planned for, making an application nearly as concrete as mathematical logic, almost completely eliminating chaos from the equation (exp: solar flares, WWIII). A program is the essence of order, I cannot fathom the number of events that have to take place in order for you to see this webpage. Baring hardware failures and changes in supporting software, this app in its current state will always behave exactly the same way.
